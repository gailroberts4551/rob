3 Tips Every Real Estate Investing Beginner Needs To Follow

Mar 5, 2018 | Real Estate Investing |

Real estate investing for beginners tips can take you further than you can imagine. Every successful investor started with some wise words to get where he is today. Most investors find it a challenge to start in the real estate business because they don’t know where to begin. Here’s what you need to know to get going.

Real Estate Investing for Beginners Tips | Start It Right

In this article:

  • Tip 1: Shop for the Best Loan
  • Tip 2: Location is Everything
  • Tip 3: Have a Real Estate Investment Strategy

 

Tip 1: Shop for the Best Loan

If you decide to invest in real estate, you’ll need a good amount of money to buy a property. You will also need it to renovate if you already have the investment property. Loans are one of the cornerstones of financing your investment. You can get a loan from the bank or private lender. If you’re from the military, you can apply for a VA loan.

Taking the risk is never easy, so always do your research when it comes to borrowing money. You’ve heard how several credit checks ding your credit in a negative way, but reports have shown that credit reports pulled within 30 days are only counted once. So if you’re going to look for the lowest APR loan, make sure you do it all at the same time. That way, your credit report only gets recorded once.

On the other hand, if securing a loan seems impossible, try real estate crowdfunding. Investing in real estate today is easier because of these new methods of financing. You may buy a rental property, such as a single-family home through crowdfunding. You can find many real estate crowdfunding platforms online.

A third party of experts also pools small amounts of money from various investors. The profit from this method is a bit smaller than traditional investing, but the cash flow is more stable and the risks are more calculated.

Finally, REITs are also an option. Real estate investors who prefer doing their investments with REITs usually have more luck finding properties. Going for investment trusts is like having a pool of the best real estate agents and advisors do the work for you.

Tip 2: Location is Everything

When looking for a real estate property, location is one of the most important things to consider. Before you start securing a real estate investment loan, make sure you know the real estate market you want to invest in.

Most properties with a premium location are quite expensive, but you can still find a reasonable property located on a great street if the building is in poor condition. It’s one fundamental piece of advice you’ll learn from real estate investors — successful real estate investments are often thanks to good locations.

The advantage of investing in a fixer-upper is the low purchase price paired with the high potential resale or rent potential. Your asset is the location; the renovation is the liability. You may spend a lot for house improvement, but you’re more likely to get a higher ROI compared to building a new house.

Tip 3: Have a Real Estate Investment Strategy

There are many ways to buy a property investment. You can build and sell a turn-key home, lease a property, build a commercial building, or rent a property. All these can be effective in generating passive income.

The most popular strategy in real estate investment is to rent out your property. There are a few strategies you can use when renting out your property, such as the following:

  • Short-term rentals
  • Airbnb rentals
  • Fully-furnished corporate rentals
  • Long-term rentals

All these will depend on the location of your investment property.

If you’re curious about how much money you need to invest in rental space, you can always seek advice from financial advisors. Do extensive research and follow their advice. For now, consider these tips as a guide to your success in real estate investment.

Real estate investment is not just for the rich and famous. It is also a good kick-start for small investors. It is a very promising way to earn passive income. Always make sure you do your homework and have the right information before you start investing.
